Discover Our Concept

Virasat, as the name inspires a legacy, works towards preserving it for the generations to come and be aware about its enriching history. One of the pioneers in treasuring the heritage of a diverse state like Rajasthan, Virasat proudly dedicates itself into the world of culture, tradition and cuisine. Situated in the heart of the city it combines the charms of the past with modern comfort highlighting the royal lifestyle from the cultural labyrinth of the Rajasthani and Indian country life. Virasat also translates and compliments a flawless and beautiful coalition of cultures that goes back a few hundred years ago and preserves it in the most stunning manner.

Heritage, luxury and royalty together sing a beautiful medley at the best Rajasthani restaurant in Jaipur. Virasat’s traditional menu is keeping the royal food legacy alive. From the mouthwatering dal bati churma to mutton rogan josh, food lovers get to enjoy the delicacies of the Marwari cuisine in a truly royal setting.

You eat with your eyes first before taking the actual first bite. At Virasat, the guests get to taste a varied and exquisite platter of Rajasthani food which is served in pure gold and silver thals. The best heritage restaurant in Jaipur has 4 restaurants and one lounge. You can choose to dine at the Sheesh Mahal the Swarn Mahal, Rajwada, Bajot Shivir or can simply relax at the Sultanat Lounge.

For those who want to dine in a royal setting but still like to try different cuisines, Rajwada offers North Indian, South Indian and Continental food as well. Catering to the food preferences of the guests is what Virasat prides itself on. Treating the guests as Gods has been a tradition in India for centuries now. Guests are welcomed with pomp and show and are treated with utmost respect. When guests visit any of Virasat’s restaurants, they are welcomed in a special style that varies between all the restaurants. Not just the welcome is grand, guests get to enjoy live folk music while they dine. It doesn’t get more royal than Virasat.

Whether it is a 11-course meal at the Sheesh Mahal or the washing of feet (as was done for gods), at the royal restaurants, Virasat is an epitome of luxury, legacy, heritage and beauty all combined in one. Guests leave with a satisfied soul and mesmerized eyes.
